Improving customer segmentation with clustering techniques

The email marketing team has had no success with their email campaigns. They are planning next year's strategies and recognize they are not clear about who their actual customers are, so I will help them find out with some clustering techniques.

Models, techniques and libraries used on this project:

  • EDA, including univariate and bivariate analyses, feature engineering and data processing.
  • Preparing data for segmentation: separating behavioural customer data from the columns that will be used for profiling (demographic data).
  • Scaling the data and applying PCA and T-SNE.
  • Clustering models: K-Means, K-Medoids, Hierarchical clustering, DBSCAN and Gaussian Mixture Model.
  • Python libraries used: NumPy, Pandas, Sklearn, Matplotlib, Seaborn.

ABSTRACT

After four campaigns and almost no engagement, doing some customer research is needed to better understand what are the segments in the customers that the store already has, and how to reach new ones. With these project, I found out they have four customer segments, clearly differentiated by customers' behaviour regarding the type of product they consume and amount spent.

BACKGROUND AND QUESTIONS

The aim of thi project is to help them find the best possible customer segmentation. This project was my capstone project to complete the Applied data science program I took at MIT Professional Education during 2022.
